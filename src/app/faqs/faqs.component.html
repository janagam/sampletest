Generic Medicines

Q.What are generic medicines?

Answer

Generic medicines are defined as “a drug product that is comparable to a brand/reference listed drug product in dosage form, strength, quality, performance, and intended use."

Q.Describe briefly the origin of a generic drug and when does it come to the market?

Answer

The Research and Development Departments in pharmaceutical and medicine manufacturing firms are mainly responsible for starting their drug discovery, development, and finally marketing the new patent drugs after the approval of the FDA (Food and Drug Administration). The patent drug usually lasts for some years to give the company a chance to recoup its research investment. Once the patent drug completes its period in the market, a generic version of the same drug is made available in the market. Generics are marketed under the drug's chemical or generic name and meet the same FDA quality and standards of the original drug.

Q.Why are generic drugs safe and effective as brand drugs?

Answer

Generic drugs have been approved by the FDA to be safe and effective as brand drugs because they

Contain the same active ingredients.

They are identical in strength, dosage form, and route of administration.

Have the same indications, dosing and labeling.

Provides the same efficacy and safety profile to patients and hence called to be “bioequivalent”.

Q.Are generic drugs strong as brand named drugs?

Answer

Yes! Generic drugs are strong as brand named drugs. They have been approved by the FDA to have same quantity, strength and stability as brand named drugs.

Q.Do generic drugs take longer to work in the body than the brand drugs?

Answer

No, generic drugs work in the same way and in the same amount of time as the brand named drugs.

Q.Explain why generic drugs are less expensive than brand drugs?

Answer

The generic drugs are less expensive than the brand drugs because they do not have the investment cost of research, development, and marketing. Generic drug manufacturers rarely spend money on advertising and marketing which is another important way of keeping the costs down. Brand drug manufacturers invest billions of dollars in research, development and marketing their drugs to the doctors in order to make their drug public.

Q.Are brand named drugs made better factors than generic drugs?

Answer

No. Generic drugs meet the same standards as the brand drugs because they have been approved by the FDA.

Q.Are generic drugs made by the same companies that make brand-name drugs?

Answer

In some cases, Yes. Brand-named companies make about half of the generic drugs.

Q.Is a generic drug available for my brand-named prescription?

Answer

Yes! A generic drug is available for the brand named prescription.
